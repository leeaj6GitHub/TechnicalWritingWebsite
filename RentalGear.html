Rental Gear Setups

  • Research rental options. Explore local ski shops, resort rental shops, or online rental services to find a convenient and reliable provider.
  • Make reservations. Book your ski gear rental in advance, especially during peak seasons, to ensure availability and secure the equipment you need.
  • Know your measurements. Provide accurate height, weight, shoe size, and skiing ability information when making reservations to ensure proper fitting equipment.
  • Choose your package. Select a rental package based on your skill level and preferences, which may include options for beginner, intermediate, or advanced skiers, as well as more specialized equipment for skiing.
  • Arrive early. Arrive at the rental shop early on your rental day to allow enough time for fitting, adjustments, and orientation with the equipment.
  • Communicate your needs. Inform the rental technician of any special requirements, preferences, or concerns you have regarding the equipment, such as specific boot sizes, binding settings, or equipment modifications.
  • Try before you go. Test the equipment on snow or carpet. You should test boots, skis, bindings, and poles, to ensure proper fit, comfort, and functionality before hitting the slopes.
  • Inspect the gear. Thoroughly inspect the rental equipment for any damage, defects, or missing parts, and notify the rental shop staff immediately if you encounter any issues.
  • Understand rental policies. Familiarize yourself with the rental shop's policies regarding equipment returns, damage waivers, insurance coverage, late fees, and other terms and conditions to avoid any misunderstandings or additional charges.
  • Return on time. Return the rental equipment on the agreed-upon date and time to avoid late fees or penalties, and ensure that all items are clean and in good condition as per the rental agreement.
