How to Ski

  1. Stand upright. Position yourself with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent, keeping your weight slightly forward over the middle of your skis.
  2. Hold your ski poles. Grip your ski poles firmly with your hands, placing the straps around your wrists to prevent dropping them.
  3. Start sliding. Push off gently with your poles or use a slight downhill slope to begin moving forward, maintaining your balance as you glide.
  4. Stop safely.
    • To stop, gradually apply pressure to the inside edges of both skis by shifting your weight backward slightly and bringing the tips closer together until you form a wedge.
    • Hold this position to stop, or move on to step 5 to begin turning.
  5. Practice turning.
    • To turn left, shift your weight slightly onto your right ski while pushing the edge of your left ski into the snow, guiding yourself in the desired direction.
    • To turn right, reverse this process by shifting your weight onto your left ski and pushing the edge of your right ski into the snow.
  6. Control your speed.
    • Control your speed by adjusting the angle of your skis in the wedge and using your poles for balance and stability.
    • Turning more frequently will cause you to go slower, while turning less frequently will make you go faster.
    • Making “S” shapes in the snow with your turning will help to keep you from going too fast.
  7. Practice falling. If you lose your balance and fall, try to relax and roll onto your side to minimize impact. Use your poles to push yourself up and resume skiing.
  8. Practice, practice, practice. Continue practicing basic techniques such as turning, stopping, and controlling your speed until you feel comfortable and confident on your skis.
