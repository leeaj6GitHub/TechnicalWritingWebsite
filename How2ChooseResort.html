How to Choose a Resort

Consider skill level

Choose a resort that offers slopes suitable for your skiing abilities, whether you're a beginner, intermediate, or advanced.

Terrain variety

Look for resorts with diverse terrain, including groomed trails, off-piste areas, moguls, and terrain parks, to cater to different preferences and skill levels.

Snow conditions

Research the resort's average snowfall, snow quality, and snowmaking capabilities to ensure optimal skiing conditions during your visit.

Resort amenities

Evaluate the amenities available, such as ski-in/ski-out lodging, dining options, equipment rentals, ski schools, childcare services, and apr√®s-ski activities to enhance your overall experience.

Accessibility

Consider the resort's location in relation to your travel arrangements, including proximity to airports, ease of transportation, and parking availability.

Crowd levels

Check reviews or visit during non-peak times to avoid overcrowded slopes and long lift lines, especially if you prefer a quieter skiing experience.

Budget

Compare lift ticket prices, accommodation rates, and other expenses to ensure the resort aligns with your budgetary constraints.

Family-friendly features

If traveling with family, look for resorts with beginner-friendly slopes, children's programs, and family-oriented activities to accommodate everyone's needs.

Special interests

Seek out resorts that cater to specific interests, such as backcountry skiing, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, or snowmobiling, if you have particular preferences or hobbies.
